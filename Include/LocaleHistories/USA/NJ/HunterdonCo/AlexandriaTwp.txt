Alexandria Township, New Jersey is located in scenic Hunterdon County in
western New Jersey.

Settled by Europeans in the early 1700s, the township was named for James
Alexander who purchased 10,000 acres in this locale in 1744.  Alexandria
includes the historic villages of Everittstown, Mount Pleasant, Little York
and part of Pittstown.  Measuring 28.1 square miles, the township presently
[2004] has less than 5,000 residents.  Farming still plays an important
role in the life of residents.  Crop, dairy and horse farms continue to
dot the countryside.
